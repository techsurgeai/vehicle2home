CASE STUDY:

Smart Charging Reduces Fleet Energy Costs

March 6, 2026

A regional delivery fleet operating in a high-demand urban area struggled with rising electricity costs after transitioning to electric vehicles. Initially, vehicles were charged immediately after returning to the depot, often during peak tariff hours. To address this, the company implemented a smart charging system that scheduled charging during off-peak periods when electricity rates were significantly lower.

By integrating time-of-use pricing into their charging strategy, the fleet was able to optimize when energy was consumed without disrupting operations. Additionally, centralized monitoring allowed operators to ensure all vehicles were fully charged before deployment. Within months, the fleet experienced a noticeable reduction in overall energy expenses while maintaining operational efficiency.

Key Takeaway: Smart charging transforms energy costs from a fixed expense into a controllable, optimized variable.
